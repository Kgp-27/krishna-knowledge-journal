# Krishna's Knowledge Journal Vision

Version: 1.0
Status: Active

# Purpose

Krishna's Knowledge Journal exists to build a permanent body of knowledge that explains finance, economics, Indian banking, taxation, and history from first principles.

The objective is not merely to provide information, but to help readers develop a deep understanding of how institutions, markets, and economic systems function.

---

# Audience

This journal is written for:

- Students
- Lifelong learners
- Professionals seeking conceptual clarity
- Anyone curious about finance and economics

No prior expertise is assumed.

---

# Long-Term Vision

Krishna's Knowledge Journal is intended to become a long-term intellectual asset rather than a traditional blog.

Over time it should evolve into a comprehensive knowledge library containing interconnected articles that remain relevant for years.

The project prioritizes quality, accuracy, and durability over publishing frequency.

---

# Scope

The journal focuses exclusively on five permanent pillars:

1. Finance
2. Economics
3. Indian Banking
4. Taxation
5. History

New content should strengthen one or more of these pillars.

---

# Writing Philosophy

Every article should:

- Explain before concluding.
- Teach from first principles.
- Build understanding instead of memorization.
- Connect ideas across disciplines.
- Remain evergreen whenever possible.

---

# Success Criteria

The project is successful when:

- Readers gain genuine understanding.
- Articles remain valuable years after publication.
- Publishing remains simple and sustainable.
- The project can be maintained by one person.
- Knowledge grows without increasing unnecessary complexity.

---

# What This Project Is Not

Krishna's Knowledge Journal is not:

- A news website
- A content farm
- A social media platform
- A technology showcase
- A framework experiment

Technology exists only to support the knowledge.

---

# Vision Statement

To create one of the most trusted, well-organized, and enduring online resources for understanding finance, economics, banking, taxation, and history through clear, first-principles thinking.
