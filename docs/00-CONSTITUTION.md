# Krishna's Knowledge Journal Constitution

Version: 1.0
Status: Active

## Mission

Krishna's Knowledge Journal exists to explain finance, economics, Indian banking, taxation, and history through first-principles thinking.

The goal is to help readers understand systems, not memorize conclusions.

---

## Vision

Build a permanent, trustworthy knowledge library that remains useful, maintainable, and understandable for decades.

---

## Core Principles

1. Knowledge before technology.
2. Content before presentation.
3. Understanding before memorization.
4. Simplicity before complexity.
5. Long-term maintainability over short-term convenience.

---

## Non-Negotiables

### Knowledge

- Markdown is the single source of truth.
- Knowledge must remain portable.
- Content is the primary asset.

### Architecture

- Architecture is designed before implementation.
- Architecture changes only between milestones.
- Every architectural decision must be documented.

### Development

- Every milestone ends with a working project.
- Every milestone ends with a Git commit.
- Never redesign while implementing.

### Technology

- Static before dynamic.
- Every dependency must solve a present problem.
- No infrastructure before it is needed.

### Writing

- Writing always takes priority over features.
- Evergreen content is preferred over news-driven content.
- Every article should teach from first principles.

---

## Definition of Success

The project is successful if, ten years from now:

- Publishing remains simple.
- Maintenance remains minimal.
- The knowledge remains valuable.
- The architecture remains understandable by one person.

---

## Amendment Rule

This Constitution should change only when the project's mission changes, not because of new tools, frameworks, or trends.
