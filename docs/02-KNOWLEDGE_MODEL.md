# Knowledge Model

Version: 1.0
Status: Active

# Purpose

The Knowledge Model defines how information is organized within Krishna's Knowledge Journal.

Knowledge is the foundation of the project.

The website is only one way of presenting that knowledge.

---

# Knowledge Hierarchy

Every piece of content follows this hierarchy:

Pillar
→ Topic
→ Article

Relationships exist between articles through tags and related concepts.

---

# Permanent Pillars

These pillars are permanent and should change only under exceptional circumstances.

1. Finance
2. Economics
3. Indian Banking
4. Taxation
5. History

---

# Topics

Topics are subdivisions within each pillar.

Examples:

Finance
- Time Value of Money
- Financial Statements
- Bonds
- Equity
- Risk Management

Economics
- Demand & Supply
- Inflation
- GDP
- Monetary Policy
- Fiscal Policy

Indian Banking
- RBI
- Commercial Banks
- Money Market
- Payment Systems
- Financial Regulation

Taxation
- Income Tax
- GST
- Corporate Tax
- Tax Administration

History
- Monetary History
- Economic History
- Banking History
- Financial Crises
- Institutions

Topics may evolve over time.

---

# Articles

An article is the fundamental unit of knowledge.

Each article explains one primary concept.

Every article belongs to:

- One Pillar (required)
- One Topic (required)
- Multiple Tags (optional)

---

# Tags

Tags provide cross-connections between articles.

Examples:

- RBI
- Inflation
- Repo Rate
- GDP
- GST
- Banking
- Budget
- Monetary Policy

Tags should be concise and reusable.

---

# Related Articles

Articles may reference related articles.

Example:

Understanding Inflation

Related:

- Money Supply
- Repo Rate
- CPI vs WPI
- Monetary Policy

Relationships should improve learning rather than SEO.

---

# Learning Philosophy

Readers should be able to progress naturally.

Simple concepts should lead to more advanced concepts.

Knowledge should feel interconnected rather than isolated.

---

# Naming Rules

- One concept per article.
- Clear titles.
- Avoid clickbait.
- Avoid unnecessary abbreviations.
- Prefer evergreen terminology.

---

# Future Expansion

Future features may include:

- Series
- Reading paths
- Automatic related articles
- Search
- Knowledge visualization

These features are optional.

The Knowledge Model must remain valid even without them.

---

# Guiding Principle

Knowledge is organized for understanding, not for technology.

The organization of knowledge must never depend on the framework, database, or website implementation.
